TIMELINE INFOGRAPHIC

How Technology Helped us Learn and Educate ourselves more Faster and more Convenient for this new Generation

1990
School Integration of Computers
Typing/word processing occurred as basic computer labs were introduced within schools. Educational software was created via floppy disk and CD-ROMs (e.g., Encarta, Reader Rabbit).
2000
School Integration of the Internet
Internet access was provided in schools and homes. Research shifted to Google; email and forums enabled collaboration with teachers and peers.
2005
Video/Multimedia Based Learning
YouTube introduced free educational content. Smartboards replaced chalkboards. Multimedia tools like PowerPoint became standard.
2010
Mobile Learning/Cloud Based Tools
Smartphones and apps (Khan Academy, Quizlet) became learning tools. Google Docs and Classrooms enabled collaboration in real-time.
2015
Online Learning Options Abound
Platforms like Edmodo and Canvas became common. MOOCs (Coursera, edX) offered access to quality education. Students built digital portfolios.
2020
Pandemic Move to Remote Learning
Zoom, Google Meet enabled online classes. Lessons became more flexible through screen shares, breakout rooms, and digital whiteboards.
2025
AI/Immersive Learning (Emerging)
AI tools like ChatGPT helped with writing and research. VR and AR are starting to be used for immersive educational experiences.
